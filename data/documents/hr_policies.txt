## 1. Employment Policies

### Equal Employment Opportunity
TechCorp is an equal opportunity employer. We do not discriminate based on race, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, national origin, age, disability, veteran status, or any other protected characteristic.

### At-Will Employment
Employment at TechCorp is at-will, meaning either the employee or the company may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause or notice.

### Background Checks
All offers of employment are contingent upon successful completion of background checks, including:
- Criminal background check
- Employment verification
- Education verification
- Reference checks
- Drug screening (where permitted by law)

### Onboarding Process
New employees complete a comprehensive onboarding program:
- **Week 1**: HR orientation, IT setup, benefits enrollment
- **Week 2**: Department-specific training
- **Week 3-4**: Project assignment and mentorship
- **30-60-90 Day Check-ins**: Regular meetings with manager

### Probationary Period
All new hires serve a 90-day probationary period during which performance is closely monitored. Either party may terminate employment during this period with minimal notice.

## 2. Compensation and Benefits

### Salary Structure

**Pay Grades and Ranges:**

| Level | Title | Salary Range |
|-------|-------|--------------|
| L1 | Junior/Entry Level | $70,000 - $100,000 |
| L2 | Mid-Level | $100,000 - $140,000 |
| L3 | Senior | $140,000 - $190,000 |
| L4 | Staff/Principal | $190,000 - $250,000 |
| L5 | Senior Staff/Distinguished | $250,000 - $350,000 |
| M1 | Manager | $150,000 - $200,000 |
| M2 | Senior Manager | $200,000 - $270,000 |
| M3 | Director | $270,000 - $350,000 |
| M4 | VP | $350,000 - $500,000 |
| E1 | Executive | $500,000+ |

### Payroll Schedule
- Employees are paid bi-weekly on alternating Fridays
- Direct deposit is required for all employees
- Pay stubs are available electronically via ADP portal

### Annual Performance Reviews
- Conducted every January
- Merit increases range from 0-10% based on performance
- Performance ratings: Exceeds (10%), Meets (7%), Needs Improvement (3%), Unsatisfactory (0%)

### Equity Compensation
All full-time employees receive stock options:
- Entry Level (L1-L2): 1,000 - 5,000 options
- Mid-Level (L3): 5,000 - 15,000 options
- Senior (L4-L5): 15,000 - 50,000 options
- Managers: 20,000 - 100,000 options
- Directors/VPs: 100,000 - 500,000 options
- Executives: 500,000+ options

**Vesting Schedule**: 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff
- 25% vests after 1 year
- Remaining 75% vests monthly over 3 years

### Health Insurance
**Medical Insurance:**
- Company pays 90% of premium for employee coverage
- Company pays 70% of premium for dependent coverage
- Plans: PPO, HMO, High Deductible Health Plan (HDHP)
- Annual deductibles: $500 (individual), $1,500 (family)

**Dental Insurance:**
- Company pays 100% of premium for employee
- Company pays 50% for dependents
- Coverage includes preventive, basic, and major services

**Vision Insurance:**
- Company pays 100% of premium
- Annual eye exam covered
- $200 allowance for frames/lenses every 2 years

### Retirement Benefits
**401(k) Plan:**
- Eligible after 30 days of employment
- Company matches 100% up to 6% of salary
- Immediate vesting of employee contributions
- Company match vests over 3 years (33% per year)
- Investment options through Fidelity

### Additional Benefits
- **Life Insurance**: 2x annual salary (company-paid)
- **Disability Insurance**: Short-term (60% salary) and long-term (60% salary)
- **FSA**: Healthcare and Dependent Care flexible spending accounts
- **HSA**: Available with HDHP, company contributes $1,000/year
- **Commuter Benefits**: Pre-tax transit and parking up to IRS limits
- **Employee Assistance Program**: Free confidential counseling services

## 3. Time Off and Leave

### Paid Time Off (PTO)
**Unlimited PTO Policy:**
- Employees may take time off as needed with manager approval
- Minimum 15 days recommended annually
- Must coordinate with team to ensure coverage
- Requires 2 weeks notice for absences of 1 week or more

### Holidays
TechCorp observes 12 paid holidays annually:
1. New Year's Day
2. Martin Luther King Jr. Day
3. Presidents' Day
4. Memorial Day
5. Juneteenth
6. Independence Day
7. Labor Day
8. Thanksgiving Day
9. Day after Thanksgiving
10. Christmas Eve
11. Christmas Day
12. New Year's Eve

**Floating Holidays**: 2 additional days per year for personal/religious observance

### Sick Leave
- Unlimited sick leave with manager notification
- Doctor's note required for absences exceeding 3 consecutive days
- Can be used for personal illness or care of immediate family

### Parental Leave
**Birth/Adoption Leave:**
- **Primary caregiver**: 16 weeks paid leave
- **Secondary caregiver**: 8 weeks paid leave
- Must be taken within 12 months of birth/adoption
- Job protection guaranteed

### Bereavement Leave
- **Immediate family**: Up to 5 days paid leave
- **Extended family**: Up to 3 days paid leave
- Immediate family: Spouse, partner, child, parent, sibling
- Extended family: Grandparent, grandchild, in-laws

### Jury Duty
- Unlimited paid time off for jury duty
- Must provide jury summons documentation

### Sabbatical Program
Employees with 5+ years of service are eligible for:
- **4-week paid sabbatical** after 5 years
- **6-week paid sabbatical** after 10 years
- Purpose: Rest, recharge, pursue personal projects
- Requires 3 months advance notice

## 4. Performance Management

### Performance Review Cycle
**Annual Reviews (January):**
- Self-assessment submission
- Manager evaluation
- Peer feedback (360-degree review for managers)
- Calibration meetings
- Final rating and compensation decisions

**Mid-Year Check-in (July):**
- Progress review
- Goal adjustment
- Development plan update

### Performance Ratings
1. **Exceptional (E)**: Far exceeds expectations (Top 5%)
   - Eligible for 8-10% merit increase
   - Promotion consideration
   - Bonus: 20-30% of salary

2. **Exceeds Expectations (EE)**: Consistently exceeds goals (15-20%)
   - Eligible for 5-7% merit increase
   - Bonus: 15-20% of salary

3. **Meets Expectations (ME)**: Achieves all goals (65-70%)
   - Eligible for 3-5% merit increase
   - Bonus: 10-15% of salary

4. **Needs Improvement (NI)**: Some goals not met (5-10%)
   - Eligible for 0-2% merit increase
   - Performance improvement plan required
   - Bonus: 0-5% of salary

5. **Unsatisfactory (U)**: Significant performance issues (<5%)
   - No merit increase
   - No bonus
   - May result in termination

### Goal Setting
- **OKRs (Objectives and Key Results)** methodology
- Individual goals aligned with team and company objectives
- Quarterly goal reviews
- SMART goals: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound

### Performance Improvement Plans (PIP)
- Triggered by "Needs Improvement" or "Unsatisfactory" rating
- 60-90 day improvement period
- Weekly check-ins with manager
- Specific, measurable goals
- HR involvement throughout process
- Outcomes: Successful completion, extension, or termination

## 5. Code of Conduct

### Professional Behavior
Employees are expected to:
- Treat colleagues with respect and dignity
- Communicate professionally and constructively
- Maintain confidentiality of sensitive information
- Avoid conflicts of interest
- Report ethical violations

### Anti-Harassment Policy
TechCorp has zero tolerance for:
- Sexual harassment
- Discrimination
- Bullying or intimidation
- Retaliation against reporters

**Reporting**: Contact HR immediately or use anonymous hotline: 1-800-TECHCORP

### Confidentiality and Data Security
- Protect company intellectual property
- Do not share confidential information externally
- Use strong passwords and two-factor authentication
- Report security incidents immediately
- Sign NDA and IP assignment agreements

### Social Media Policy
- Personal social media should not represent company views
- Do not share confidential company information
- Be respectful when discussing work-related topics
- Include disclaimer: "Views are my own"

### Dress Code
**Business Casual:**
- Acceptable: Jeans, casual pants, collared shirts, blouses, sweaters
- Avoid: Tank tops, shorts, flip-flops, torn clothing
- Client meetings: Business professional attire
- Fridays: Casual (company t-shirts encouraged)

### Workplace Safety
- Report safety hazards immediately
- Participate in emergency drills
- Know evacuation routes
- First aid kits available on each floor
- Report accidents within 24 hours

## 6. Remote Work Policy

### Eligibility
- Must have manager approval
- Minimum 6 months tenure
- Demonstrated ability to work independently
- Job function suitable for remote work

### Work Arrangements
**Hybrid (3 days office, 2 days remote):**
- Most common arrangement
- Core office days: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- Flexible Monday/Friday

**Fully Remote:**
- Available for specific roles
- Must attend quarterly in-person meetings
- May be required to relocate near an office for some positions

**Equipment Provided:**
- Laptop
- Monitor (up to 2)
- Keyboard and mouse
- Headset
- Stipend: $500 for home office setup

### Expectations
- Available during core hours (10 AM - 4 PM local time)
- Respond to messages within 2 hours
- Attend all required meetings
- Maintain secure home office environment
- Use VPN for company network access

### Communication
- Daily standup (video)
- Slack for team communication
- Calendar must be kept up-to-date
- Camera-on for team meetings

## 8. Termination and Resignation

### Voluntary Resignation
- **Notice Period**: 2 weeks standard, 4 weeks for managers/senior roles
- Submit written resignation to manager and HR
- Exit interview conducted
- Return all company property
- Final paycheck includes unused PTO payout

### Involuntary Termination
**Grounds for immediate termination:**
- Gross misconduct
- Theft or fraud
- Violence or threats
- Violation of company policies
- Breach of confidentiality

**Layoffs/Reduction in Force:**
- Minimum 60 days notice (WARN Act compliance)
- Severance package based on tenure:
  - <1 year: 4 weeks
  - 1-3 years: 8 weeks
  - 3-5 years: 12 weeks
  - 5+ years: 16 weeks
- Extended health benefits (COBRA)
- Outplacement services provided

### Exit Process
1. Exit interview with HR
2. Return equipment (laptop, badge, keys)
3. Final expense report submission
4. Transfer of work responsibilities
5. Deactivation of accounts and access
6. Final paycheck processing
7. COBRA notification

### Post-Employment
- Non-compete agreement: 6 months (varies by state)
- Non-solicitation: 12 months (no recruiting TechCorp employees/clients)
- Continued confidentiality obligations
- Stock option exercise period: 90 days post-termination

### Alumni Network
- Former employees invited to join alumni network
- Quarterly networking events
- Job referral bonuses still apply
- Rehire eligibility after 6 months
